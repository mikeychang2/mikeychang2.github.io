Reflections on Building my Personal Site

Learning through Failing

December 1, 2014

Working on my personal website for DBC has been a challenging learning experience. Without any prior HTML/CSS experience, I was thrown into the first challenge on the second week of our Phase 0 period to create a page that would serve as our online entity - the jumpstart and virtual resume to our new careers as programmers. Immersion tends to be force you to fail quickly - and learn as a result. The first day working on my website took a lot time, more than expected. I went down a few rabbit holes, playing around with color scheme and fonts, spacing and sizing. I couldn’t quite get the positioning of objects. And some links and images didn’t turn out quite how I wanted them too.

Fast forward a couple of weeks. My site looks a bit closer to how I visualized it in my head. In some ways, it’s exactly how I wanted it to appear. And in some ways, it differs drastically. However, I’ve learned that flexibility and adaptability are vital in programming and learning new languages. So, my site in its current manifestation is just fine.

Overall, it’s been a great experience. Plus, it chronicles my thoughts throughout the experience about both technical culture and technical concepts, so I see my growth. This week, in tweaking my website this week, I fleshed out some of the pages and debugged some of the links. I debated whether or not to change the entire format of the website, mostly font and color scheme, but I decided against going down that rabbit hole. At least for now, I’m still liking the colors...
